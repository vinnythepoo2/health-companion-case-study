Health Companion

A Super App for Managing Chronic Health Conditions

Vincent | UX/UI Design Course | 2026

Problem Statement

People with chronic conditions face significant challenges in managing their health: they forget medications, miss appointments, and feel overwhelmed managing health information across multiple platforms. Current health apps are often confusing, visually overwhelming, and don't adequately support communication between patients, caregivers, and healthcare professionals.

Goal: Help users remember medications, track intake, manage appointments, and communicate with healthcare professionals through a simple, intuitive interface designed for people with chronic conditions, their caregivers, and healthcare providers.

Research & Discovery

To understand the needs of our users, I conducted primary research through an online survey and complemented it with secondary research. A 10-question survey was distributed to 15 participants with chronic conditions, exploring medication adherence, appointment management, and current use of health apps. Existing studies on medication adherence and chronic disease management were also reviewed.

Key Research Insights

  • 68% reported forgetting medications when routines changed or during travel
  • 52% found existing health apps confusing or visually overwhelming
  • Participants preferred clear, simple reminders over complex dashboards
  • Inconsistent appointment notifications caused stress and anxiety
  • Users desired a simple way to send non-urgent messages to healthcare providers

These insights guided our design decisions, emphasizing clear interfaces, one-tap confirmations, caregiver access, and optional advanced features for power users.

Low-fidelity planning was initially used to determine layout hierarchy before refining the visual structure. Key design decisions included:

  • Bottom Navigation Structure: Ensuring consistent orientation across all main sections (Home, Medication, Appointments, Messages, Profile)
  • Card-Based Layout: Using cards to reduce cognitive overload and maintain visual cohesion

  • Clear Status Indicators: Visual confirmation states to reduce ambiguity about medication intake

Usability Testing

To evaluate the effectiveness of the Health Companion prototype, a formative usability test was planned focusing on task completion, navigation clarity, and information hierarchy. The goal was to determine whether users can easily manage medications, view appointments, and communicate with healthcare professionals using the bottom navigation structure and card-based interface.

Testing Methodology

The test would involve 5â€“7 participants representing individuals managing chronic conditions, such as Type 2 Diabetes or hypertension (similar to persona Marie Tremblay). Participants would interact with the clickable Figma prototype on a mobile-sized display (390 Ã— 844).

Test Tasks

  • Check today's medication status on the Home screen
  • Navigate to the Medication screen and mark a medication as taken
  • Set an alarm in the Calendar & Alarm section
  • View details of the next appointment
  • Open a message conversation with a doctor
  • Locate notification settings within the Profile screen

Observation Focus

A think-aloud method would be used during testing to understand users' thought processes. Observations would focus on:

  • Whether users understand status labels such as "Status: Today" and green confirmation indicators
  • Whether they can easily identify the "Confirm" button in the alarm setup
  • Whether the bottom navigation icons are intuitive
  • Task completion time and navigation errors
  • Visual clarity of the dark interface and text readability

Expected Findings & Iterations

Feedback would be analyzed by identifying recurring usability issues, such as confusion about labels, difficulty locating features, or visual overload in sections like the calendar.

Planned Improvements: Increasing contrast for important buttons, enlarging confirmation indicators, simplifying calendar controls, and clarifying tab icons with stronger labels. The prototype would then be refined and retested to validate improvements.

Iterations & Improvements

The design process involved continuous refinement based on research insights, persona needs, and anticipated usability feedback. Several key challenges required iterative solutions to balance visual appeal with usability.

Key Design Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Dark Theme Readability

Initial design had insufficient contrast between text and background, making it difficult for older users like Marie to read

Solution: Enhanced Contrast

Adjusted text colors and button contrast to maintain dark aesthetic while improving accessibility and readability

Challenge: Medication Status Ambiguity

Users couldn't immediately tell whether medication was taken or still pending without reading detailed text

Solution: Clear Visual Confirmation

Implemented clear labels and visual confirmation states with color coding to reduce ambiguity

Design Decision: Role-Based Access

Healthcare professionals and caregivers have admin privileges to manage data, while patients have standard user access to prevent accidental deletions

Rationale: Safety & Control

Why This Matters:

  • Prevents accidental data loss by patients
  • Allows caregivers to manage medication schedules
  • Gives healthcare providers oversight capabilities
  • Maintains data integrity for medical records

These iterative improvements ensured that the design remained aligned with user needs, particularly for the primary persona Marie Tremblay, who requires large buttons, clear feedback, and minimal information per screen. The refinements also addressed the needs of caregivers like Sophie who need quick status visibility without technical complexity.

Final Design

The final design represents a balance between aesthetic appeal and functional simplicity. The Health Companion app uses a clean card-based layout with a persistent bottom navigation bar, ensuring users can always access key features: Home, Medication, Appointments, Messages, and Profile.

Design Highlights

  • Bottom Navigation: Consistent orientation across all screens, easy thumb access for mobile users
  • Home Dashboard: Prioritizes "Today's medication status" followed by next appointment for immediate visibility
  • Medication Cards: Individual cards display dosage, time, and clear status indicators with one-tap confirmation
  • Calendar Integration: Simplified alarm setup with clear "Confirm" action buttons
  • Messaging System: Separate conversations for doctors, nurses, and family with conversation previews
  • Profile Management: Centralizes user information, health details, notification settings, caregiver access, and privacy options
  • Dark Theme: Modern, calming aesthetic with carefully balanced contrast for readability

Reflection & Learnings

The UX design process significantly shaped the development of the Health Companion application by ensuring that user needs remained central throughout the project. Beginning with persona creation helped clarify that the primary user may not be highly technical and may experience cognitive overload when presented with too much medical data. This insight directly influenced the decision to use a clean card-based layout, a persistent bottom navigation bar, and clear confirmation buttons instead of complex dashboards or analytics-heavy screens.

Key Design Challenges

One key challenge during the design process was balancing visual appeal with usability. The dark theme creates a modern and calming aesthetic, but it required careful attention to contrast and readability to ensure text and buttons remain accessible. Iterative adjustments were necessary to maintain clarity while preserving the visual consistency of the interface.

Another challenge involved structuring the Calendar & Alarm feature so that it did not feel overwhelming. Health-related scheduling can easily become complex, so the design focused on limiting visible options and emphasizing a single clear "Confirm" action.

Designing medication status indicators also required refinement. It was important that users immediately understand whether a medication was taken or still pending. Through iteration, the use of clear labels and visual confirmation states improved feedback and reduced ambiguity.

Key Takeaways

  • User-centered design prevents feature bloat: Rather than focusing solely on features, the process emphasized clarity, accessibility, and reassurance
  • Persona-driven decisions: Every design choice was validated against the needs of Marie (simplicity), Alex (customization), Sophie (caregiver visibility), and Dr. Cohen (concise data)
  • Accessibility from the start: Contrast, font size, and button placement were considerations from initial wireframes, not afterthoughts
  • Bottom navigation ensures consistency: Implementing bottom navigation minimized cognitive load and provided reliable orientation
  • Iterative refinement is essential: Visual appeal cannot compromise usability; continuous testing and adjustment are necessary

Impact of the UX Process

Overall, the UX design process encouraged structured thinking, continuous evaluation, and user-centered decision-making. The final prototype is intuitive, realistic, and aligned with the needs of individuals managing chronic health conditions. The design successfully addresses the core problem: helping users remember medications, track intake, manage appointments, and communicate with healthcare professionals without feeling overwhelmed.
